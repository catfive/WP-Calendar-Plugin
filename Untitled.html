Simian Virus 40 (SV 40)

    In 1960 the SV 40 virus was discovered in the Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine and the Oral Polio Vaccine by Dr. Fernanda Martini. SV 40 is a polyomavirus isolated from the rhesus monkey kidney cell cultures. The virus is found in diseases mesothelioma, osteosarcoma and ependymoma. In 1961 the U.S. government passed regulations requiring that all polio vaccines to be free of SV 40. However stocks of vaccine produced before 1961 were not recalled, and so polio vaccines contaminated with SV 40 could have been used until 1963.
